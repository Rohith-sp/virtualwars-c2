How Elections Work

The Election Timeline

📢
Announcement
Election Commission announces election dates. Model Code of Conduct kicks in immediately — government cannot announce new schemes or transfers.
📝
Nominations
Candidates file their nomination papers. The Returning Officer scrutinises each nomination for eligibility and completeness.
📋
Withdrawal
Last date for candidates to withdraw from the race. The final candidate list is published and symbols are allotted.
📣
Campaign Period
Parties campaign actively through rallies, advertising, and canvassing. Model Code of Conduct is strictly enforced throughout.
🤫
Campaign Silence
48 hours before polling, all campaigning stops. No rallies, no political ads, no social media campaigns during this period.
🗳️
Polling Day
Voters cast their ballots. Booths open 7 AM to 6 PM. Indelible ink marks your finger. Carry at least one approved photo ID.
🏆
Vote Counting
Ballots are counted at designated counting centres under observer supervision. Results are declared and winners take oath of office.

Electoral Forms Explained

Every Form You Need to Know

🆕
Form 6

New Voter Registration

⏱ 30–45 days

Eligible if: Indian citizen, 18+ years old by January 1 of the reference year

Documents Required
  • •Age Proof — Aadhaar, Birth Certificate, or School Certificate
  • •Address Proof — Aadhaar, Utility Bill, or Bank Passbook
  • •Recent passport-size photograph
Apply Online →
🗑️
Form 7

Deletion of Voter Entry

⏱ 15–30 days

Eligible if: Any citizen can file on behalf of a deceased or relocated voter

Documents Required
  • •Death certificate for deceased voter (municipal or hospital)
  • •Proof of permanent relocation (utility bill, Aadhaar at new address)
  • •Supporting evidence for bogus or duplicate entries
Apply Online →
✏️
Form 8

Correction in Voter Details

⏱ 15–30 days

Eligible if: Any registered voter who needs to update their electoral entry

Documents Required
  • •Proof of correct detail — Aadhaar, PAN card, or Birth Certificate
  • •Recent photograph (for photo correction)
  • •New address proof (for address correction)
Apply Online →
🔄
Form 8A

Transposition Within Constituency

⏱ 10–20 days

Eligible if: Voters who have moved to a new address within the same Assembly constituency

Documents Required
  • •New address proof within same constituency — Aadhaar or Utility Bill
  • •Your current EPIC card number
Apply Online →

Common Issues

Got a Problem? We've Got the Fix

✏️Medium
Name on voter card doesn't match Aadhaar
File Form 8 for name correction. Upload your Aadhaar as proof of the correct name. Processing takes 15–30 days.
Get help →
🔍Urgent
My name is missing from the electoral roll
Search at voters.eci.gov.in to confirm. If genuinely missing, file Form 6 immediately. On election day, approach the ERO for a Tender Vote.
Get help →
📸Low
My voter ID has a very old or blurry photo
File Form 8 for photo correction. Bring a new recent passport-size photograph. The updated card is issued after verification.
Get help →
⚠️Urgent
I'm registered in two different constituencies
File Form 7 to delete one entry. By law, you can only be registered in one constituency — your current residential address.
Get help →
🏠Medium
Voter ID shows my old address after moving
If within the same constituency, use Form 8A (transposition — fastest option). If you've moved to a new constituency, file Form 7 + Form 6.
Get help →
💳Low
My Voter ID card is damaged or torn
Apply for a duplicate EPIC at voters.eci.gov.in. Pay the Rs 25 fee. While waiting, the e-EPIC digital card on the Voter Helpline App is legally valid at booths.
Get help →
